# Geological and Palaeontological Time/Stratigraphic Terms

In strict stratigraphy, the usual geological terms have two parallel forms:

| Rock/time-rock unit | Time unit |
|---|---|
| **Eonothem** | **Eon** |
| **Erathem** | **Era** |
| **System** | **Period** |
| **Series** | **Epoch** |
| **Stage** | **Age** |

For example:

**Mesozoic Erathem** = the rocks deposited during the **Mesozoic Era**  
**Jurassic System** = the rocks deposited during the **Jurassic Period**  
**Oxfordian Stage** = the rocks deposited during the **Oxfordian Age**

---

## Palaeontological Equivalents

For **palaeontology**, there is not a perfect one-to-one equivalent to:

**eon → era → period → epoch → age**

Palaeontology usually uses **biostratigraphic** or **biochronological** units based on fossils.

The closest palaeontological equivalents are:

| Geological time/rock level | Palaeontological equivalent idea |
|---|---|
| **Eon / Era** | Very broad evolutionary interval, such as “Age of Reptiles” or “Age of Mammals” — informal, not usually formal stratigraphy |
| **Period / System** | Major faunal/floral interval, for example “Cambrian trilobite faunas” or “Cretaceous dinosaur faunas” |
| **Epoch / Series** | Faunal stage, mammal age, regional fossil assemblage interval |
| **Age / Stage** | **Biozone**, **assemblage zone**, **range zone**, or **taxon zone** |

---

## Biozones

The most formal palaeontological units are **biozones**. These are intervals of rock defined by fossil evidence.

Common biozone types include:

| Biozone type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Range zone** | Defined by the stratigraphic range of a particular fossil taxon |
| **Concurrent range zone** | Defined by the overlap of two or more taxa |
| **Assemblage zone** | Defined by a distinctive group of fossils occurring together |
| **Abundance zone** | Defined by a fossil taxon being especially common |
| **Interval zone** | Defined between two fossil events, such as first or last appearances |
| **Lineage zone** | Defined by part of an evolutionary lineage |

---

## Vertebrate Palaeontology Terms

In vertebrate palaeontology, you may also see terms such as:

| Term | Example / Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Land Mammal Age** | North American Land Mammal Ages, such as Wasatchian, Bridgerian, Barstovian |
| **Faunal stage** | A regional interval characterised by a particular fossil fauna |
| **Faunal assemblage** | A distinctive set of animals found together |
| **Faunal zone** | A zone defined by characteristic fossils |

---

## Simple Summary

**Geology uses eon, era, period/system, epoch/series, age/stage.**

**Palaeontology usually uses fossil-based units such as faunas, assemblages, faunal stages, land mammal ages, and especially biozones.**
